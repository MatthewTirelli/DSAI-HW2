# Executive Summary of High-Risk Patient Cohort Findings

## Overall Patterns in the Cohort
The cohort comprises 26 patients identified as high-risk based on recent screening scores and safety indicators. The majority of patients exhibit elevated PHQ-9 scores, indicating significant depressive symptoms, with scores ranging from 20 to 27. Notably, several patients have multiple diagnoses, including anxiety disorders, depressive disorders, and ADHD, suggesting a complex interplay of mental health conditions.

## Provider Involvement and Access Trends
A diverse range of providers is involved in the care of this cohort, with several providers seeing multiple patients. Notably, Dr. Murphy has the highest number of visits, indicating a potential concentration of care. However, many patients have not had follow-up visits within the recommended timeframe, raising concerns about continuity of care. The average days since the last visit across the cohort is approximately 188 days, with some patients exceeding 500 days since their last appointment.

## Medication or Documentation Themes
The cohort exhibits a variety of medication regimens, with notable mentions of:
- **Aripiprazole** and **Lamotrigine** being the most frequently prescribed medications.
- A significant number of patients are documented as receiving no medications, which may indicate either a treatment gap or a preference for non-pharmacological interventions.
- Other commonly prescribed medications include **Lithium**, **Fluoxetine**, and **Vyvanse**.

This diversity in medication usage reflects the varied diagnoses and treatment approaches within the cohort.

## Notable Concerns Related to Follow-Up or Care Continuity
Several patients have not had a follow-up visit in over 180 days, with the longest lapse exceeding 500 days. This raises significant concerns regarding care continuity and the potential for worsening mental health conditions. The lack of follow-up could lead to increased risk of adverse outcomes, highlighting the need for improved tracking and engagement strategies for high-risk patients.

In summary, while the cohort demonstrates a range of treatment approaches and provider involvement, the significant gaps in follow-up care warrant immediate attention to ensure that patients receive the necessary support and interventions for their mental health needs.
